# Lazy Office Brand Video Style

This file is the stable style source for Kevin's daily Lazy Office video factory.

## Brand

- Brand name: 偷懶辦公室 / LAZY OFFICE
- Positioning: AI 落地師 for SMEs, helping teams turn existing workflows into practical AI employees.
- Core message: 不賣冷冰冰的 AI 軟體；先理解企業痛點，再把重複工作交給可落地的數位員工。
- Tone: 親切、幽默、實作導向、讓人想把工作變輕鬆。

## Visual Vibe

- American cute illustration.
- Notion hand-drawn notebook vibe.
- Bright, humorous automation-life aesthetic.
- Friendly, problem-solving, lightly funny. Avoid cold enterprise SaaS visuals.
- Avoid dark gray corporate realism, formal stock photos, and stiff business line art.

## Layout

- White background with subtle light-gray notebook grid pattern.
- Bento-box cards with thick dark-brown rounded borders and soft shadows.
- Decorative masking tape, hand-drawn arrows, sticky notes, and speech bubbles.
- Sloth mascot should appear in most scenes, roughly 30-40% of the frame when appropriate.
- Keep text-safe areas clear and readable.

## Color Palette

- Background: white `#FFFFFF` with subtle grid.
- Main accents: warm yellow/orange.
- Secondary accents: mint green, tech blue, soft purple/lavender.
- Text: dark coffee brown or deep blue-gray, not pure black.

## Typography Direction

- Chinese title: Noto Sans TC Bold style.
- English: rounded sans-serif feel.
- Keywords may use highlighter effects or colorful blocks.
- Body text should feel like clean Noto Sans TC with comfortable line height.

## Character And Iconography

- Core mascot: sloth character.
- Allowed sloth roles: pajamas, office chair, consultant suit/glasses, teacher, traveler, detective, robot/cardboard automation worker.
- Illustration style: vector-like, bold outlines, flat colors.
- Scene elements: sofa, coffee, laptop, workflow diagrams, chat windows, LINE automation, spreadsheets, invoices, OCR, dashboard, AI employees.

## Image Prompt Base

Use this base prompt for both aspect ratios, adapting composition for the selected format:

```text
American cute illustration, Notion hand-drawn notebook vibe, bright white background with subtle gray grid pattern, thick dark-brown rounded bento card borders, masking tape, soft shadows, warm yellow, mint green, tech blue, soft purple accents, Lazy Office sloth mascot, premium but humorous office automation aesthetic, vector-like bold outlines, clean title-safe and subtitle-safe areas, readable visual hierarchy, no gibberish text, no watermark, no real company logos.
```

## Aspect Ratio Rules

- Generate both formats for the same selected topic and the same script:
  - 16:9 landscape for YouTube / presentation / desktop.
  - 9:16 vertical for Reels / Shorts / TikTok / LINE.
- Do not create multiple topics in one run.
- Use the same 3-5 scene narrative across both formats.
- 16:9 composition: slide-like horizontal bento layout, room for title and 2-3 short callouts.
- 9:16 composition: stacked cards, larger mascot, fewer words, strong opening hook.

## Daily Video Structure

For each morning/evening selected topic:

Default to 4 scenes:

1. Opening hook: a branded 1-2 sentence cold open in the first 3 seconds.
2. Plain-language news: what changed, translated into work language.
3. Work scenario: who should care and what repeated work can be reduced.
4. Judgment plus CTA: what to test first, who should wait, and one clear Lazy Office branded action.

Use a 5th scene only when the topic has a meaningful limitation, risk, cost, permission, accuracy, or regional availability issue that viewers should know before testing.

Keep each video focused on exactly one selected news/project.

## Opening Rules

- The opening is not a generic news headline. It should sound like an AI landing consultant helping busy teams save time.
- Lead with the practical implication, then mention the news context.
- The first 3 seconds must answer at least two of these:
  - Who should care: owner, manager, marketing, sales, customer support, admin, content team, builder.
  - What job it affects: writing, replying, organizing, slides, workflows, development, reporting.
  - What decision it supports: worth testing, wait and watch, saves steps, lowers friction, reduces repeated work.
- Preferred opening formula:
  - `今天這則 AI 消息，最值得注意的是＿＿＿。如果你是＿＿＿，你要看的是它能不能幫你＿＿＿。`
- Alternate formulas:
  - `這則新聞重點不在技術多厲害，而是你今天能不能拿它少做＿＿＿。`
  - `如果你最近正在處理＿＿＿，那今天這則 AI 新聞你可以特別看一下。`
- Keep it conversational and specific. Avoid anchor/newsreader phrasing.

## CTA Rules

- CTA should extend the video idea into a next practical action.
- Prefer interaction and workflow discovery over generic like/follow requests.
- Use one primary CTA per video.
- Preferred CTA formula:
  - `如果你想看我把這題拆成實際用法，留言＿＿＿。我再幫你整理成你工作上用得到的版本。`
- Good CTA options:
  - 留言「想看實作」.
  - 留言你的產業或工作場景.
  - 先收藏，之後要導入 AI 流程再回來看.
  - 想看每天早晚 AI 重點，追蹤偷懶辦公室.
- Avoid hard-cut CTAs that ignore the topic. Avoid using the same CTA every time.

## Narration Voice

- Speak like a practical friend who understands AI and wants to save the viewer time.
- Use plain Traditional Chinese, with light humor and a clear judgment.
- Translate technical terms into work language.
- Useful sentence patterns:
  - `白話翻譯一下，這代表＿＿＿。`
  - `對＿＿＿的人來說，這會比較有感。`
  - `我的判斷是：＿＿＿。`
  - `不是看它炫不炫，是看你能不能少做幾步。`
- Avoid pure newsreading, hype, FOMO, and technical term dumping.

## Do Not

- Do not make the video feel like a generic AI news broadcast.
- Do not only say the model/tool is stronger, faster, or bigger without a concrete work use case.
- Do not assume every viewer is an engineer.
- Do not let cute visuals reduce clarity or cover key text.
- Do not end with only `like and subscribe`; tie the CTA to the selected topic and the viewer's workflow.
